Introduction back to ToC

A large part of activity in construction and renovation projects is focused on the creation of information (requirements, designs, plans, contracts) and the existence of suitable information is a prerequisite for the execution of most activities.

Digital Construction Information ontology mostly extends the class dice:InformationContentEntity, defined in the Digital Construction Entities ontology as a subclass of generally dependent continuant and aligned with the concept information content entity of the Information Artifact Ontology. The essential property of information content entity is "aboutness": each dice:InformationContentEntity isAbout some other entity. For example, a BIM model is about a building, or a master plan is about a project.

Due to the fast development of devices and infrastructure to get access to information, it is nowadays becoming increasingly unclear and less important to know where the information is physically stored, as long as it can be accessed. For this reason, the exclusive focus in this ontology is on information contents, not the physical carriers (paper, memory card, hard disk, cloud storage, or a specific server).
